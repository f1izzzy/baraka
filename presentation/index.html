Problem

Retail brands need measurable offline conversion, not just views.

What stores have now

  • Instagram posts and stories
  • Manual replies in Telegram and DMs
  • No clear signal on which offer brings someone in
  • No clean merchant-side redemption flow

What Baraka changes

  • One Mini App for deal discovery
  • One QR flow for in-store redemption
  • One merchant panel for confirmation
  • One admin dashboard for store-level insight
Product

A simple system built around one real outcome: getting people into stores.

01

Mini App

Users discover stores, activate items, and prepare a store-level QR.

02

QR Activation

The platform creates a short-lived payload for a single store order.

03

Merchant Redemption

The merchant scans the QR and confirms redemption in-store.

04

Admin Intelligence

The brand sees catalog activity, redeem history, merchants, analytics, and audit.

Current MVP

This MVP already proves the full product loop.

What already works

  • Telegram Mini App customer flow
  • Store and product management
  • Favorites and activations
  • QR generation and redeem flow
  • Merchant store-based login
  • Admin analytics and audit log

What this means

We are not pitching a concept. We already have a working MVP with real flow.

It validates demand, merchant workflow, and measurable offline retail action.

Why Telegram First

Telegram was the fastest and smartest way to validate the market.

Why the product starts here

Telegram lets Baraka validate the full customer-to-store loop fast and cheaply before native development.

This is a strategic MVP decision, not a limitation story.

What the MVP already validated

  • Users can discover and activate local offers
  • Merchants can redeem offers in-store with low friction
  • Stores can manage catalog and merchant access centrally
  • Offline retail intent can be measured, not guessed
